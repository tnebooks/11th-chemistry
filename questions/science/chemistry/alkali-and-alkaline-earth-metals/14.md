**Assertion: Generally alkali and alkaline earth metals form superoxides. Reason: There is a single bond between O and O in superoxides.**

```markdown
Assertion is false - only the larger alkali metals (K, Rb, Cs) form superoxides; Li and Na do not. Alkaline earth metals generally do not form superoxides. Reason is also false - in superoxides (O₂⁻), the O-O bond order is 1.5 (not a single bond), with one unpaired electron. Both assertion and reason are false.
Correct answer is (d).
```
