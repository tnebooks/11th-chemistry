**The product obtained as a result of a reaction of nitrogen with CaC₂ is (NEET - Phase I)**

```markdown
Calcium carbide (CaC₂) reacts with nitrogen at high temperature to form calcium cyanamide (CaCN₂), which is written as Ca(CN)₂ in some contexts. Actually the product is calcium cyanamide: CaC₂ + N₂ → CaCN₂ + C. The formula is often written as Ca(CN)₂.
Correct answer is (c) Ca(CN)₂.
```
