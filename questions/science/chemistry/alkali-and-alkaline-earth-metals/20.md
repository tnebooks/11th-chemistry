**The compound (X) on heating gives a colourless gas and a residue that is dissolved in water to obtain (B). Excess of CO₂ is bubbled through aqueous solution of B, C is formed. Solid (C) on heating gives back X. (B) is**

```markdown
CaCO₃ (X) on heating gives CaO (residue) + CO₂. CaO dissolves in water to give Ca(OH)₂ (B). Excess CO₂ + Ca(OH)₂ → Ca(HCO₃)₂ (C). Ca(HCO₃)₂ on heating gives CaCO₃ (X) back. So B is Ca(OH)₂.
Correct answer is (b) Ca(OH)₂.
```
