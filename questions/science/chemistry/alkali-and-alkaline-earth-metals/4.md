**Which of the following has the highest tendency to give the reaction M⁺(g) → M⁺(aq)?**

```markdown
The reaction M⁺(g) → M⁺(aq) represents hydration of gaseous cation. The tendency for hydration depends on charge density (charge/radius ratio). Li⁺ has the smallest size and highest charge density among alkali metal cations, so it has the highest hydration energy and highest tendency to undergo hydration.
Correct answer is (b) Li.
```
