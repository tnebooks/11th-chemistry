**Al(OH)₃ + 3HCl(aq) → AlCl₃ + 3H₂O. How many millilitres of 0.1 M Al(OH)₃ solution are needed to neutralise 21 mL of 0.1 M HCl?**

```markdown
Al(OH)₃ provides 3 OH⁻ ions per formula unit.
Millimoles of HCl = 0.1 × 21 = 2.1 mmol
Moles of OH⁻ needed = 2.1 mmol
Volume of 0.1 M Al(OH)₃ = 2.1/(3 × 0.1) = 7 mL
Correct answer is (b) 7 mL.
```
