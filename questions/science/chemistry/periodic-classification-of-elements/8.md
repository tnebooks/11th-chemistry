**Identify the wrong statement**

```markdown
In isoelectronic species, smaller positive charge means weaker nuclear attraction and larger ionic radius (not smaller). For example, Na⁺ (11) < Mg²⁺ (12) - but both have 10 electrons; Na⁺ is larger than Mg²⁺. So "smaller positive charge = smaller ionic radius" is wrong. Other statements are correct: higher negative charge = larger ionic radius; atomic radius increases down the group; atomic radius decreases across a period.
Correct answer is (a).
```
