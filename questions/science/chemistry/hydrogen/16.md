**The hybridisation of oxygen atom in H₂O and H₂O₂ are, respectively**

```markdown
In H₂O, oxygen is sp³ hybridised (two lone pairs and two bond pairs, bent shape). In H₂O₂, oxygen is also sp³ hybridised (two lone pairs and two bond pairs on each oxygen, with a skew structure). Both are sp³ hybridised.
Correct answer is (d) sp³ and sp³.
```
