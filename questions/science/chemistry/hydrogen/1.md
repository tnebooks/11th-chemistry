**Which of the following statements about hydrogen is incorrect? (NEET - 2016)**

```markdown
Hydrogen has three isotopes: protium (¹H), deuterium (²H), and tritium (³H). Protium is the most common isotope (99.98%), not tritium. Tritium is radioactive and found in trace amounts. Other statements are correct: H₃O⁺ exists in solution, H₂ acts as a reducing agent, and H⁺ acts as cation in ionic salts (e.g., HCl).
Correct answer is (c).
```
