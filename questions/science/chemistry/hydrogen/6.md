**Non-stoichiometric hydrides are formed by**

```markdown
Non-stoichiometric hydrides (interstitial hydrides) are formed by transition metals like palladium, vanadium, titanium, zirconium, etc. These hydrides do not have a fixed composition (e.g., PdH₀.₆, VH₀.₅). They are formed by d-block elements that can absorb hydrogen into their crystal lattice.
Correct answer is (a) palladium, vanadium.
```
