**The amount of heat exchanged with the surrounding at constant pressure is given by the quantity**

```markdown
At constant pressure, the heat exchanged with the surroundings is equal to the change in enthalpy (ΔH). From the first law of thermodynamics: qₚ = ΔH. ΔE is the change in internal energy (at constant volume). ΔS is entropy change, and ΔG is Gibbs free energy change.
Correct answer is (b) ΔH.
```
