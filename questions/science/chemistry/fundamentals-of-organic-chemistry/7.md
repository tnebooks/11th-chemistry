**The IUPAC name of CH₃CH=CH-C≡CH is**

```markdown
Longest chain containing both double and triple bonds has 5 carbons (pent). Number from the end nearest to the multiple bonds. The triple bond gets priority over double bond for numbering, but both should get lowest numbers. CH₃CH=CH-C≡CH → numbering from right gives: C-1 is CH₃, C-2 is CH, C-3 is CH, C-4 is C, C-5 is CH. Double bond at C-2? Actually CH₃-CH=CH-C≡CH → from right: CH₃ (C1)-CH(C2)=CH(C3)-C(C4)≡CH(C5). So double bond at C-2, triple at C-4. Name: pent-2-en-4-yne. But priority: ene before yne. So pent-2-en-4-yne. However option (b) is pent-3-en-1-yne. Let's number from left: CH₃ (C5)-CH(C4)=CH(C3)-C(C2)≡CH(C1). Double bond at C-3, triple at C-1 → pent-3-en-1-yne. Since triple bond gets priority, numbering from right gives lower number to triple bond (C-1). So pent-3-en-1-yne is correct.
Correct answer is (b).
```
