**A sample of 0.5g of an organic compound was treated according to Kjeldahl's method. The ammonia evolved was absorbed in 50mL of 0.5M H₂SO₄. The remaining acid after neutralisation by ammonia consumed 80mL of 0.5M NaOH. The percentage of nitrogen in the organic compound is**

```markdown
Total millimoles of H₂SO₄ = 50 × 0.5 = 25 mmol
Millimoles of NaOH used = 80 × 0.5 = 40 mmol
Millimoles of NH₃ = 2 × (25 - 40/2) = 2 × (25 - 20) = 10 mmol? Wait:
H₂SO₄ + 2NH₃ → (NH₄)₂SO₄
H₂SO₄ + 2NaOH → Na₂SO₄ + 2H₂O
Millimoles of H₂SO₄ neutralized by NH₃ = (50×0.5) - (80×0.5/2) = 25 - 20 = 5 mmol
Millimoles of NH₃ = 2 × 5 = 10 mmol
Moles of N = 10 × 10⁻³ mol
Mass of N = 10 × 10⁻³ × 14 = 0.14 g
Percentage of N = (0.14/0.5) × 100 = 28%
Correct answer is (b) 28%.
```
