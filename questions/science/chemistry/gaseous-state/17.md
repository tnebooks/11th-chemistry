**Equal moles of hydrogen and oxygen gases are placed in a container, with a pin-hole through which both can escape. What fraction of oxygen escapes in the time required for one-half of the hydrogen to escape? (NEET phase I)**

```markdown
Graham's law: r ∝ 1/√M.
r(O₂)/r(H₂) = √(M_H₂/M_O₂) = √(2/32) = √(1/16) = 1/4.
In the time required for 1/2 mole H₂ to escape, O₂ escaped = (1/4) × (1/2) = 1/8 mole.
Fraction of O₂ escaped = (1/8)/1 = 1/8.
Correct answer is (c) 1/8.
```
