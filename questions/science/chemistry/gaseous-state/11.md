**Use of hot air balloon in sports and meteorological observation is an application of**

```markdown
Hot air balloons work on the principle that hot air is less dense than cold air. This is based on Charles's law (volume increases with temperature at constant pressure) and Boyle's law (pressure-volume relationship). The buoyant force is due to density difference. It is primarily an application of Charles's law. Among options, Boyle's law is the closest related to gas behavior in balloons.
Correct answer is (a) Boyle's law.
```
