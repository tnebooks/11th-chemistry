**Assertion: Critical temperature of CO₂ is 304K, it can be liquefied above 304K. Reason: For a given mass of gas, volume is directly proportional to pressure at constant temperature.**

```markdown
Assertion is false - CO₂ can be liquefied only below its critical temperature (304K), not above it. Above critical temperature, gas cannot be liquefied regardless of pressure. Reason is also false - at constant temperature, volume is inversely proportional to pressure (Boyle's law), not directly proportional.
Correct answer is (d) both assertion and reason are false.
```
