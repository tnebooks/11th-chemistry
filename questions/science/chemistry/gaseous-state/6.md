**The temperature at which real gases obey the ideal gas laws over a wide range of pressure is called**

```markdown
The temperature at which a real gas obeys ideal gas behavior over a wide range of pressure is called the Boyle temperature (or Boyle point). At this temperature, the gas shows minimum deviation from ideality. Critical temperature is the temperature above which gas cannot be liquefied.
Correct answer is (b) Boyle temperature.
```
