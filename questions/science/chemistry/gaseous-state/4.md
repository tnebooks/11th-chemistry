**When an ideal gas undergoes unrestrained expansion, no cooling occurs because the molecules**

```markdown
In unrestrained (free) expansion of an ideal gas into vacuum, no work is done and no cooling occurs. This is because ideal gas molecules exert no attractive forces on each other. There is no intermolecular attraction, so no energy is required to overcome them.
Correct answer is (b).
```
